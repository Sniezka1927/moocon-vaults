# Moocon Vaults

Tokenized yield vaults on Solana built on **Jupiter Lend**. Deposit any supported token, receive a transferable position token (pToken) representing your vault share, and enter provably-fair prize drawings every round. Yield generated by depositors is pooled and paid out to a single winner each round.

---

## What it does

1. **Deposit** USDC or WSOL (soon any supported token by Jupiter Lend program) — funds flow into Jupiter Lending and earn yield continuously.
2. **Receive pTokens** — position tokens representing your share of the vault.
3. **Every round**, a drawing selects a winner from all current depositors using verifiable on-chain randomness powered by **ORAO VRF**.
4. **Winner claims** the round's prize.
5. **Withdraw anytime** — redeem pTokens for the base deposited Tokens.

Referral codes and a points system sit on top for user growth incentives.

---

## How randomness works

No participant — including the protocol — can know or influence who wins before it happens. The drawing uses a **Commit–Reveal scheme** layered with **ORAO VRF** so that the result depends on two independent entropy sources that must both be present before a winner can be derived.

**Step 1 — Snapshot**

Every eligible vault position is captured into a Merkle tree at round start. Each depositor's wallet and ticket weight is included. The Merkle root is committed to the smart contract and visible on-chain.

**Step 2 — Commit**

The operator hashes a secret and submits it to the contract, triggering a VRF request to ORAO's external validator network. From this point, the secret is locked in. 

**Step 3 — Reveal**

Once VRF fulfillment lands on-chain, the operator publishes the secret. The two values are XOR-combined into a final seed, which runs through the KISS algorithm to pick a winner index. Anyone can verify the result against their Merkle proof.

### What financial problem does this solve?

Lending yield is safe and boring. Anything actually exciting requires putting principal at risk. There's no middle ground.

Moocon Vaults pools yield across all depositors and pays it out as a single prize each round. Your principal stays intact. 

Prize savings products have existed in traditional finance for decades with hundreds of billions locked in them. On-chain, the concept is still early — and existing implementations are limited to native SOL staking. Moocon Vaults is the first to open it up to any supported token, stablecoins included.

### Why Solana?

The mechanism only works if the operational cost of running it is low enough to be worth it for small depositors, and if round cadence is fast enough to feel alive.

**Cost.** Each round involves a VRF request, a Merkle root commit, a reveal, and individual claim transactions. Sub-cent Solana fees make the whole thing economically viable at any scale.

**Speed.** Drawings run every 60 minutes. The full commit-reveal cycle — snapshot, VRF fulfillment, reveal — needs to settle cleanly within that window. Solana's block times make this routine.

**Yield infrastructure.** Jupiter Lending is the yield source.

### Who is this for?

**Token holders who want to earn more** — whether their assets are sitting idle or already earning a base rate somewhere else. The prize mechanic gives them a reason to stay engaged without changing their risk profile.

**Users beyond the SOL-staking ecosystem.** The architecture here is built on lending vaults. Allowing to stake not only SOL but also stablecoins without keeping exposure to price asset.
